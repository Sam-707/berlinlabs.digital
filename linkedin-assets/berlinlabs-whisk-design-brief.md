# BerlinLabs — Whisk Logo Design Brief

---

## BRAND IDENTITY

**Name:** BerlinLabs
**Positioning:** "A small product studio based in Berlin. We build digital products — and sometimes help others build theirs."
**Core values:** Calm, premium, minimal, Berlin-quality craft

---

## THE WHISK CONCEPT

### Why a Whisk?
A whisk represents:
- **Mixing ingredients** → combining code, design, strategy
- **Creating something new** → products from raw ideas
- **Craft & precision** → intentional work, not chaos
- **Kitchen tool** → building products like cooking (process + art)
- **Simple tool, great results** → minimal tools, maximum outcome

### Design Direction
- **NOT** a cartoonish or literal kitchen whisk
- **Abstract, geometric, premium**
- Think: icon that hints at whisk shape through wire loops and handle
- Minimal lines, confident curves

---

## VISUAL DIRECTION

### Style References
- **Stripe** — clean, geometric, premium
- **Linear** — dark, modern, tech-forward
- **Figma** — playful but professional
- **Notion** — minimal, typography-first

### Color Palette
```
Primary Dark: #1a1a1a (near-black)
Primary Accent: #d4af37 (gold/brass — premium, Berlin feel)
Secondary: #2a2a2a (dark gray)

```

### Typography
- **Headings:** Bebas Neue (bold, tall, confident)
- **Body:** Manrope (clean, modern, readable)
- **Monospace:** JetBrains Mono or similar (for tech accents)

## LOGO REQUIREMENTS

### Primary Logo (Whisk Icon)
**Format:** SVG
**Size:** Scalable (optimized for 300×300px LinkedIn profile)

**Design specs:**
- Stylized whisk silhouette
- 3-4 wire loops (suggestive, not literal)
- Sleek handle (minimal detail)
- Use gold accent on ONE wire loop or tip
- Rest in dark tones
- Must work on:
  - Dark backgrounds
  - Light backgrounds
  - Favicon size (32×32px)

**Variations needed:**
1. Full color (dark + gold)
2. Monochrome (single color)
3. Icon only (whisk, no text)
4. Horizontal lockup (whisk + "BerlinLabs" text)

## DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### Do:
- Keep it minimal (less is more)
- Use whitespace generously
- Ensure legibility at small sizes
- Maintain geometric precision
- Test on dark and light backgrounds
- Use gold sparingly (accent, not dominant)

### Don't:
- Over-detail the whisk wires (abstract > literal)
- Use gradients (solid colors = more premium)
- Make it cute/playful (this is a studio, not a toy brand)
- Clutter with effects
- Use more than 2-3 colors total
