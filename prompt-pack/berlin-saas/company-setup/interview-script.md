# ICP Validation Interview Script

## Interview Logistics
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Format:** Zoom or Google Meet
- **Recording:** Ask permission first
- **Goal:** Understand if developers building SaaS on the side need a development framework

---

## Opening (2 minutes)

**Script:**
"Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time to chat with me today. I'm building something for developers who ship SaaS products, and I'm trying to understand the real challenges people face.

This isn't a sales pitch - I'm just trying to learn. Your honest feedback will help me decide what to build (or if I should even build it at all).

Do I have your permission to record this for my notes? I'll keep it private and only use it to capture your feedback accurately."

**If yes:** "Great, let's dive in."

**If no:** "No problem, I'll take detailed notes instead."

---

## Section 1: Background (5 minutes)
*Goal: Understand who they are and their current situation*

**1.** What do you do for work? How long have you been in that role?

**2.** What side projects are you working on right now, if any?

**3.** How long have you been building on the side?

**4.** Walk me through your last project. What was it? What happened to it?

**Follow-up if needed:**
- Did you launch it?
- Did you get users?
- Is it still running?
- Why did you stop?

---

## Section 2: Problem Discovery (15 minutes)
*Goal: Find the pain points*

**5.** What was the hardest part about building your last project?

**6.** How long did it take you from idea to a working prototype?

**7.** What part took the longest? Why?

**8.** Think about the projects you abandoned or didn't finish. What made you stop?

**9.** What would have helped you ship faster?

**10.** Walk me through your development process when you start something new. Where do you begin?

**11.** Do you use any templates, boilerplates, or frameworks when starting a project?

**12.** What do you copy from project to project? What do you rebuild every time?

**13.** How do you handle things like:
   - Authentication (login, signup, OAuth)
   - Payments (Stripe integration)
   - Admin dashboards
   - Real-time features

**14.** How much time do you spend on these vs. your actual product features?

**15.** What's the most frustrating part of the technical setup?

---

## Section 3: Solution Testing (10 minutes)
*Goal: Test if your solution resonates*

**16.** If you had a complete framework with:
   - Authentication patterns (email + OAuth ready)
   - Payments integrated (Stripe)
   - Real-time features built-in
   - 5+ production-ready templates to start from
   - Best practices already implemented

   How would that change your workflow?

**17.** What would you expect to pay for something like that?
   - Follow-up: Why that price?
   - Follow-up: What are you comparing it to?

**18.** What would make you NOT buy this?
   - What would need to be true for you to say no?

**19.** Would you use this for your next project? Why or why not?

**20.** Who do you think needs this most? (Who would you recommend this to?)

---

## Section 4: Demographics & Wrap-up (5 minutes)
*Goal: Understand who they are and get any additional insights*

**21.** Age range and location?

**22.** What's your primary tech stack? (Framework, database, hosting)

**23.** How many side projects have you started in the last 2 years?

**24.** How many have you launched?

**25.** What's your goal with your next project?

**26.** Is there anything else about building SaaS products that I didn't ask?

**27.** If I build this, would you be open to seeing it when it's ready?

---

## Closing

**Script:**
"This has been incredibly helpful. You've given me a lot to think about.

If it's okay with you, I might follow up with you in a few weeks once I've processed all these interviews and maybe built something. Would you be open to that?

Also, is there anything I can help you with? Code review, feedback on your project, introduction to someone? Happy to return the favor."

**If they want help:** Note what they need and make sure to follow through.

**Final:** "Thanks again, [Name]. I'll be in touch!"

## Interview Checklist

### Before the Interview
- [ ] Send calendar invite with Zoom/Google Meet link
- [ ] Test your recording setup
- [ ] Have a notepad ready for additional observations
- [ ] Review their LinkedIn/profile for context
- [ ] Note which persona you think they are

### During the Interview
- [ ] Ask permission to record
- [ ] Note exact quotes when possible
- [ ] Mark problem signals (✅) and anti-patterns (❌)
- [ ] Watch for emotional cues (excitement, frustration)
- [ ] Don't sell - just listen

### After the Interview
- [ ] Complete the interview notes template within 1 hour
- [ ] Mark which persona they fit best
- [ ] Extract 3 key quotes
- [ ] Rate: Would they use this? (Yes/No/Maybe)
- [ ] Rate: Would they pay? (Yes/No/Maybe)
- [ ] Note any follow-up actions
- [ ] Send thank-you message

## Quick Reference: Persona Signals

### Persona 1: Side-Project Builder (Primary)
- Full-time developer
- 5-10 hours/week for side projects
- Has 3-5 failed projects
- Wants to ship something that gets users

**Listen for:**
- "I keep starting over with every project"
- "Auth/payments always take me forever"
- "I have 5 half-finished projects"
- "I know how to code, not what to build"

### Persona 2: Freelancer Scaling to Products
- Freelance developer (€40-80/hour)
- Wants product income to reduce client reliance
- Knows how to build, not what to build

**Listen for:**
- "I'm tired of trading time for money"
- "I want something that earns while I sleep"
- "I can build anything, I don't know what to build"

### Persona 3: First-Time Technical Founder
- Just quit or planning to quit job
- Has 6-12 months runway
- Never shipped product before

**Listen for:**
- "I'm leaving my job to build a SaaS"
- "I have savings for X months"
- "This is my first real product"

### Anti-Patterns (Wrong ICP)
- "I use create-react-app and it's fine"
- "I don't need patterns, I build from scratch"
- "My company pays for everything"
- "I'm not technical, I'm the business person"

## Note-Taking Tips

### What to Capture
1. **Exact quotes** - Their words are more powerful than your paraphrasing
2. **Emotional moments** - When do they get excited? Frustrated?
3. **Numbers** - Time spent, money spent, projects started/launched
4. **Specific behaviors** - What do they ACTUALLY do, not what they say they do
5. **Comparisons** - What do they compare your solution to?

### What to Ignore
- Vague statements like "it would be nice"
- Features they'd never actually use
- Suggestions for "nice to have" features
- Opinions on design/branding (too early)

### Red Flags
- They're just being nice and agree with everything
- They say they'd pay but won't commit to a price
- They can't articulate a real problem
- They're not actually your target customer

---

## Counter-Questions for Deep Learning

When they give vague answers, drill deeper:

| They say | Ask instead |
|----------|-------------|
| "It would be nice to have" | "What's the most frustrating thing you do right now?" |
| "I'd probably pay something" | "What's the most you've paid for a dev tool? Why?" |
| "I'm not sure" | "Walk me through the last time you tried to do X. What happened?" |
| "It's all fine" | "What would you change if you could?" |
| "I don't have time" | "What would you make time for?" |
