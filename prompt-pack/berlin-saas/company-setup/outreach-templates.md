# Customer Discovery Outreach Templates

## Overview
Use these templates to reach out to potential interview candidates. The goal is to schedule 10 interviews from 50 outreach attempts.

**Target:** 50 people → 10 interviews (20% conversion rate)

---

## LinkedIn Outreach (20 people)

### Connection Request
```
Hi [Name], saw your work on [specific thing]. I'm also building
SaaS products on the side - would love to connect and follow your journey!
```

### First Message Template

**Subject:** Quick question about your SaaS dev process

```
Hey [Name],

Saw your post about [specific thing they posted about - be specific!].

I'm building a development framework for solo founders shipping SaaS
products, and I'm trying to understand the biggest bottlenecks.

Would you have 15 min for a quick call this week?

No sales pitch - just customer research to make sure I build something
actually useful. Happy to return the favor with code review or advice.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Follow-Up Message (3 days later)

```
Hey [Name],

Just bumping this to the top of your inbox in case it got buried.

I know you're busy, so I'll keep it short - trying to interview 10
developers about their SaaS building process, and you came to mind
as someone who'd have valuable insights.

Would 15 minutes work sometime this week?

No sales pitch, just learning.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Second Follow-Up (7 days later)

```

Hey [Name],

Last message, I promise!

If now's not a good time, totally understand. But if you're ever
up for a 15-min chat about your dev process, I'd appreciate it.

And if there's anything I can help you with (code review, feedback
on your project, intro), just let me know.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### LinkedIn Comment Strategy

Before DMing, engage with their content first:

**On their posts:**
```
Love this! I'm building something similar and [specific insight about
their post]. Would love to connect and follow your journey.
```

Wait for them to accept connection or reply, THEN send DM.

## Best Practices

### Do's ✅
- Personalize every message (mention specific posts/projects)
- Offer value in return (code review, feedback, introductions)
- Be clear it's research, not sales
- Keep messages short (under 150 words)
- Follow up 2-3 times (people get busy)
- Reply to their content before DMing
- Use their name and be friendly

### Don'ts ❌
- Don't copy-paste generic messages
- Don't sell or pitch
- Don't send mass messages
- Don't spam
- Don't get discouraged by low response rates (20% is normal)
- Don't forget to follow up
- Don't be creepy or overly familiar

### Response Rate Benchmarks
- LinkedIn DM: 10-20% response rate
- Twitter DM: 5-10% response rate
- Reddit posts: 20-30% response rate
- Personal network: 30-50% response rate
- In-person: 50-70% response rate

## Common Objections & Responses

### "I'm too busy right now"

**Response:**
```

No worries! If things open up in a few weeks, let me know.

Or if you have 5 minutes to type out your biggest bottleneck when
building SaaS, that would be super helpful too.

No pressure either way!
```

### "I'm not the right person"

**Response:**
```

No problem! Is there someone you'd recommend I reach out to?

Or if you know any developers building SaaS on the side, I'd
appreciate the introduction.

Thanks anyway!
```

### "What's in it for me?"

**Response:**
```

Great question! A few things:

1. I'll share all the findings with you - insights from 10+ devs
   about what slows them down
2. Happy to help with your project (code review, feedback, etc)
3. Early access to what I'm building (if you want it)
4. My eternal gratitude 🙏

Main thing: I just want to learn, and I'm happy to return the favor.
```

### "Just send me the questions"

**Response:**
```

I could, but I've found that a 15-min conversation is way more
valuable than written answers - you can't capture nuance and
follow-up questions in writing.

That said, if you're truly up for a call, I'll make it worth your
time with [specific offer].

Let me know!
```

## A/B Testing Your Outreach

Track which templates work best:

### Test Different Subject Lines
- "Quick question about your dev process" vs
- "Building something for SaaS developers" vs
- "15 min of your time?"

### Test Different Opening Lines
- "Saw your post about X" vs
- "Love what you're building" vs
- "Fellow dev here"

### Test Different Offers
- "Happy to return the favor" vs
- "I'll buy you coffee" vs
- "Early access to what I'm building"

Track what gets the best response rate and double down on it.
