## Quick Scoring Guide

### Problem Awareness (1-5)
- 1 = Doesn't see any problem
- 2 = Acknowledges minor issues
- 3 = Recognizes the problem exists
- 4 = Problem is clear and significant
- 5 = Problem is urgent and painful

### Solution Fit (1-5)
- 1 = Wrong solution entirely
- 2 = Not really what they need
- 3 = Might help somewhat
- 4 = Good fit for their needs
- 5 = Perfect fit, exactly what they need

### Willingness to Pay (1-5)
- 1 = Would never pay
- 2 = Might pay if very cheap
- 3 = Would pay fair price
- 4 = Would pay premium
- 5 = "I need this now, take my money"

### ICP Fit (1-5)
- 1 = Not our target at all
- 2 = Some overlap
- 3 = Partial fit
- 4 = Good match
- 5 = Perfect ICP match

## Pattern Recognition Guide

After 5+ interviews, look for:

### Green Flags (Proceed)
- 7+/10 mention same bottlenecks
- 5+/10 would pay for solution
- Clear urgency ("I need this now")
- One persona clearly resonates more
- Similar feature requests across interviews
- Price expectations in €49-99 range

### Yellow Flags (Pivot)
- Problem recognized but not urgent
- Would use but wouldn't pay
- Multiple personas, no clear winner
- Wide price range (€29-299)
- Mixed feedback on solution fit

### Red Flags (Stop)
- Don't see this as a problem
- Happy with current solutions
- "I would never pay for this"
- No clear patterns emerge
- Can't define a clear ICP

## Note-Taking Best Practices

### During the Interview
1. **Record if possible** - Get permission first
2. **Note exact quotes** - Write their actual words
3. **Mark timestamps** - Note when they say important things
4. **Track emotions** - Note excitement, frustration, hesitation
5. **Ask follow-ups** - Drill into interesting statements

### After the Interview (within 1 hour)
1. **Complete notes immediately** - Memory fades fast
2. **Extract 3 key quotes** - Most important insights
3. **Score objectively** - Don't let enthusiasm bias scores
4. **Note patterns** - Similar to previous interviews?
5. **Identify next steps** - What should we do with this?

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- ❌ Leading questions ("Don't you think X is a problem?")
- ❌ Selling during interview ("We have a solution for that!")
- ❌ Ignoring emotional cues
- ❌ Forgetting to ask about pricing
- ❌ Waiting too long to write up notes
- ❌ Only writing paraphrases, not quotes
